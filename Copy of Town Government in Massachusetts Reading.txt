There are 292 towns in Massachusetts. People who live in a town have voted to have a town form of government. Towns are run by a Select Board and either a Town Meeting or a Town Council[b].  Leaders are elected by town members every 1-3 years. 


Town governments represent all the people who live in the town. They make decisions about local laws, local projects, and how money and taxes will be spent. Leaders also use their roles to support the other boards[c] in the town, including school boards[d] and the board of health. 


Depending on whether the town is run by a Select Board and Town Meeting or a Town Council, residents of a town get to vote directly on issues or vote for leaders to do so for them.[e] Each spring, towns hold an election between March and June to vote for their leaders. Town Meetings are also held at least once a year, and most are open to all community members.

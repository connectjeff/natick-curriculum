# About the Author: Jeff Alderson

**Town Moderator, Natick, Massachusetts**

## Official Role

Jeff Alderson is the elected Town Moderator for the Town of Natick, Massachusetts. He presides over Natick's Town Meeting — the local legislative body — and makes appointments to the Finance Committee, Charter and By-Law Review Committee, Hybrid Town Meeting Committee, and other committees as required.

- **Office:** Town Moderator
- **Term began:** March 2025 (elected in the 2025 Annual Town Election)
- **Predecessor:** Frank W. Foss (served 2007–2025)
- **Phone:** 508-810-7530
- **Email:** moderator@natickma.org
- **Address:** 13 East Central Street, Town Hall, Natick, MA 01760

*Sources: Town of Natick official website (natickma.gov/642/Moderator); Natick Report, "Natick swears in newly elected officials, transfers the moderator's gavel," March 27, 2025.*

## Election and Swearing-In

Alderson was elected Town Moderator in the March 2025 Town Election. Outgoing Moderator Frank W. Foss, who had served for 18 years since May 2007, publicly endorsed Alderson as his successor, writing:

> "I hope that come the March Town Election, voters will choose for the next town moderator Jeffrey Alderson, allowing me to pass the gavel to a worthy successor. I strongly believe Jeff will carry the gavel well in Natick and will respect the rights and privileges encumbered upon his office."

At a March 26, 2025 Select Board meeting, Foss formally handed over the town meeting gavel to Alderson in a brief ceremony.

*Sources: Frank W. Foss, letter to the editor, MetroWest Daily News, January 18, 2025; Natick Report, "Natick swears in newly elected officials, transfers the moderator's gavel," March 27, 2025.*

## Military Service

Alderson joined the United States Air Force in 1998 after graduating from Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) on an Air Force ROTC scholarship. He majored in electrical and computer engineering.

During his service, he was deployed to the Middle East twice — to Prince Sultan Air Base (Saudi Arabia) and Al Udeid Air Base (Qatar) — where he helped build computer systems for intelligence operations. He left the service in 2003 with the rank of captain.

*Source: Natick Veterans Oral History Project (natickveterans.com), "Jeffrey Alderson," January 29, 2026.*

## Stewardship

In April 2026, Alderson authored a public statement on the 250th anniversary of the American Revolution, connecting Natick's town meeting tradition to the nation's founding. He highlighted that on June 20, 1776 — two weeks before the Continental Congress adopted the Declaration of Independence — the people of Natick gathered in Town Meeting and unanimously adopted their own "Resolve for Independence," pledging their support with their "lives and fortunes."

He also launched the Hybrid Town Meeting Committee in fall 2025, appointing members to study and recommend a framework for hybrid (in-person and remote) town meetings in Natick, with a target of fall 2027 for implementation.

*Sources: Natick Report, "Natick Town Moderator's statement in celebration of the 250th anniversary of the American Revolution," April 17, 2026; Natick Report, "Volunteers sought to join Natick Hybrid Town Meeting Committee," November 20, 2025.*

## Gas Leaf Blowers

### 1. Town Considering A Ban On Leaf Blowing
**Source:** Natick Town News (Sean Sullivan)
**URL:** https://www.naticktownnews.com/2024/11/27/514717/town-considering-a-ban-on-leaf-blowing

**Summary:** Reports on Natick's consideration of a gas leaf blower ban, following Concord's phased approach (full ban by 2030). The proposed Natick ban included seasonal carveouts (spring/fall cleanup periods) and exceptions for properties >1.5 acres. Landscapers testified that battery technology is not yet viable for commercial use — batteries are heavy, expensive, and short-lived. Select Board Vice-Chair Bruce Evans acknowledged environmental benefits but cited enforcement concerns (police citations) and lack of landscaper differentiation. Town Meeting voted to refer for further study.

**Position:** Neutral — presents both sides; quotes ban proponents (health/environment) and opponents (practicality, enforcement).

### 6. Let's Phase Out Gas-Powered Leaf Blowers
**Source:** EcoNatick
**URL:** https://econatick.org/actions/lets-phase-out-gas-powered-leaf-blowers/

**Summary:** Advocacy page arguing for elimination of gas leaf blowers. Key claims: one hour of commercial leaf blower use emits pollution equivalent to driving 1,100 miles. Lawn equipment in the US emitted 21,800 tons of fine particulates in 2020 (equivalent to 234M cars). Health impacts include cardiovascular disease, asthma, cancer (benzene/formaldehyde). Noise exceeds WHO standards even hundreds of feet away. Lists 11 MA communities with restrictions (Acton, Arlington, Belmont, Brookline, Cambridge, Concord, Lexington, Marblehead, Newton, Swampscott, Winchester). Encourages battery-electric alternatives and raking.

**Position:** For restriction — environmental advocacy organization pushing for phase-out.

### 10. Natick's Turf Expert Update — HS Practice Field
**Source:** Natick Report (Bob Brown)
**URL:** https://www.natickreport.com/2026/04/naticks-bonifide-turf-expert-shares-update-on-high-school-practice-field-design-process/

**Summary:** April 2026 update from Art Goodhind, Natick's Land Facilities supervisor and president of the New England Sports Turf Management Association. The NHS practice field can accommodate either surface. Natural grass: lower upfront cost, higher operational cost. Artificial turf: roughly 2x more available usage hours, supports more sports (including field hockey). Site will be displaced during construction. School Committee asked about safety of various infill types (rubber, wood fibers). Next step: School Committee picks surface, then capital request goes to Town Meeting. Reader comments raise concerns about injury rates and environmental impact of artificial turf.

**Position:** Neutral — factual reporting with balanced comparison of both options.

### 11. Just Say Yes to Natural Playing Fields
**Source:** EcoNatick
**URL:** https://econatick.org/actions/just-say-yes-to-natural-playing-fields/

**Summary:** Advocacy page opposing artificial turf. Health risks: PFAS ("forever chemicals") found on children's skin after play; surface temperatures up to 150°F (recorded by Natick Health Department); quotes Mount Sinai recommending against artificial turf. Costs: AT is 2-3x more expensive over lifecycle — Kennedy Middle School field cost $1.2M, Memorial Field resurfacing $600K. Annual cost: AT $65K-$109K vs. natural $33K-$50K (per TURI/UMass Lowell analysis). Environmental: 200-3,200 lbs microplastics per field per year; 40,000 lbs plastic + 400,000 lbs infill per field made from fossil fuels; 527 tons CO2 over lifecycle. Highlights Wayland's 3-year moratorium, Marblehead (natural since 2002), Martha's Vineyard, and Springfield managing organic grass. Petitions for a moratorium on new artificial turf.

**Position:** Against artificial turf / For natural grass — environmental advocacy organization.

### 13. Fall ATM Night #2: Grass Could Get Greener
**Source:** Natick Report (Bob Brown)
**URL:** https://www.natickreport.com/2025/10/natick-fall-annual-town-meeting-night-2-grass-could-get-greener-path-paved-for-west-natick-redevelopment/

**Summary:** October 2025 meeting coverage. Article 33 (advisory moratorium on new artificial turf) passed 78-28-3 — stronger result than 2024's referral. Dr. Roger Luckmann (UMass prof emeritus) detailed PFAS/microplastics dangers, calling plastic grass blades "forever poison pills." Joe Zaino (Natick Youth Football) argued turf availability far exceeds grass. Chris Downe (Natick Youth Soccer) said field shortages cost the program $100K/year at Elm Bank. Bruce Evans said the article could "serve as a catalyst" for a broader field study committee. Article does not affect existing turf fields (Cole Center, Memorial Field, Kennedy Middle School). Town Administrator confirmed no new field projects in capital plan for next 3 years.

**Position:** Neutral — detailed journalistic account with direct quotes from both sides.

# Speaking Script: "How Does Town Meeting Work? Two Big Decisions in Natick"

**Speaker:** Jeff Alderson (Natick Town Moderator)
**Audience:** 3rd grade students in Massachusetts
**Format:** Speaking to camera, advancing slides as topics change
**Tone:** Warm, conversational, natural — like explaining something to a classroom

---

This is a guide, not a word-for-word script. Say it in your own voice. The slides are visual aids — you don't need to read them or match them exactly.

## Flow

### Slide 1 — Title Card
Introduce yourself. Say you're the Town Moderator and you're going to explain how Town Meeting works through two real examples that happened in Natick.

### Slide 2 — What Is Town Meeting?
Explain that in Massachusetts, many towns have Town Meeting — elected members vote on big decisions, kind of like a student council for the whole town. Any resident can come to speak their mind, but only elected Town Meeting members get to vote.

### Slide 3 — The Town Moderator
Talk about what a Moderator does — keeps things fair, makes sure everyone gets a turn to speak, calls the vote. You can point out that that's your job. You don't take sides, you're like a referee.

### Slide 4 — Two Big Questions
Transition: tell them about two things Natick recently debated — gas leaf blowers and artificial turf fields.

### Slide 5 — Gas Leaf Blowers
Tell them about the proposal from EcoNatick. Why they wanted to phase out gas blowers — they're loud, they pollute, they can cause health problems. Keep it simple.

### Slide 6 — The Debate (Leaf Blowers)
Some people agreed — better for health and the environment. Some disagreed — battery blowers aren't ready yet, and landscapers worried about cost. It was a good, fair debate.

### Slide 7 — Leaf Blowers Outcome
The vote was 79 to 27 to send it to a committee for more study. So no decision yet — they decided to learn more first.

### Slide 8 — Artificial Turf
Explain that a moratorium is a pause. Some residents wanted a three-year pause on building new artificial turf fields because of health and environmental worries.

### Slide 9 — The Debate (Turf)
Share some arguments you remember from the debate. Microplastics, PFAS, field heat. But also — not enough grass fields for kids, turf can be used more. Both sides had good points.

### Slide 10 — Turf Outcome
The moratorium passed — 74 to 28. No new artificial turf fields for three years while the town studies the issue more.

### Slide 11 — What Can We Learn?
Wrap it up. Anyone can speak up. Debating is healthy. Every vote counts. That's Town Meeting.

## Tips

| Tip | |
|-----|--|
| **Pace** | Speak a little slower than normal conversation — 3rd graders need time to process |
| **Terms to define** | moratorium (a pause), Town Meeting (where residents vote), Moderator (the referee) |
| **Mistakes** | If you stumble, just keep going or do a quick retake — don't aim for perfect |
| **Length** | 2–3 minutes total |

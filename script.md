# Voiceover Script: "How Does Town Meeting Work? Two Big Decisions in Natick"

**Target audience:** 3rd grade students in Massachusetts
**Duration:** ~3 minutes (12 slides, ~15s average)
**Format:** Voiceover script — each section matches storyboard slide number

---

## [0:00] Slide 1 — Title Card
**(Music intro — 5 seconds, then fade under VO)**

Welcome to "How Does Town Meeting Work? Two Big Decisions in Natick."

---

## [0:10] Slide 2 — What Is Town Meeting?

In Massachusetts, many towns don't just let a few leaders make all the choices. Instead, they hold something called Town Meeting. Any resident can come and vote on important questions — like how to spend money, or what rules to make. Think of it like a giant classroom vote, but for the whole town.

---

## [0:28] Slide 3 — The Town Moderator

Town Meeting needs someone to keep things fair and organized. That person is the Town Moderator. In Natick, the Moderator is Jeff Alderson. His job is to make sure everyone gets a chance to speak, to explain the rules, and to call the vote at the end. He doesn't take sides — he's like a referee for the meeting.

---

## [0:45] Slide 4 — Two Big Questions

At recent Town Meetings, Natick residents debated two big questions. One was about gas-powered leaf blowers. The other was about artificial turf on sports fields. Let's see what happened.

---

## [0:55] Slide 5 — Question 1: Gas Leaf Blowers

In 2024, a group called EcoNatick asked Town Meeting to phase out gas-powered leaf blowers. They said the blowers are bad for the environment and people's health — they're loud, they pollute the air, and they make a lot of noise.

---

## [1:10] Slide 6 — Leaf Blowers: The Debate

Supporters said gas leaf blowers cause asthma and noise problems, and electric ones are better. But landscapers said battery-powered blowers just aren't strong enough yet for big jobs. Some Town Meeting members worried it was "government overreach" — telling people what tools they can use. After a long debate, the town voted to send the question to a committee for more study.

---

## [1:28] Slide 7 — Leaf Blowers: The Outcome

The vote was 79 in favor of studying it more, 27 against, and 1 person who didn't vote either way. So the leaf blower question wasn't decided yet — it was sent to a committee to gather more information.

---

## [1:38] Slide 8 — Question 2: Artificial Turf

The next year, in 2025, another big question came up. Some residents wanted a three-year pause — called a moratorium — on building any new artificial turf fields in town. Artificial turf is the plastic grass you see on some sports fields.

---

## [1:53] Slide 9 — Artificial Turf: The Debate

Dr. Roger Luckmann, a doctor from UMass, told Town Meeting that microplastics from artificial turf are ending up in our bodies and could be harmful. He said the plastic blades break down into tiny pieces that can get into the air and water. Other supporters worried about "forever chemicals" called PFAS, and said the fields get dangerously hot in summer.

But youth sports leaders said Natick doesn't have enough fields for kids to play on. Artificial turf can be used much more than grass fields, especially after rain. Some worried that a three-year pause would delay getting more playing space for kids.

---

## [2:18] Slide 10 — Artificial Turf: The Outcome

After hearing both sides, Town Meeting voted 74 to 28 to pass the three-year moratorium on new artificial turf. Three people chose not to vote. Natick will study the issue before building any more plastic fields.

---

## [2:28] Slide 11 — What Can We Learn?

So what can we learn from all this? First, in Town Meeting, anyone can bring an idea or speak up about something they care about. Second, it's okay to disagree — debating helps us make better decisions. And third, everyone's vote counts equally. That's democracy in action.

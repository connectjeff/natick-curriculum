# Speaking Script: "How Does Town Meeting Work? Two Big Decisions in Natick"

**Speaker:** Jeff Alderson (Natick Town Moderator)
**Audience:** 3rd grade students in Massachusetts
**Format:** Speaking to camera, advancing slides as topics change
**Tone:** Warm, conversational, natural — like talking to a classroom

---

This is a guide, not a word-for-word script. Say it in your own voice. The slides are visual aids — you don't need to read them or match them exactly.

---

### Slide 1 — Title Card
Introduce yourself. Say you're the Town Moderator and you're going to explain how Town Meeting works through two real votes that happened in Natick.

### Slide 2 — What Is Town Meeting?
Explain that in Massachusetts, many towns have Town Meeting. It's like a student council for the whole town. Elected members vote on big things like money, rules, and projects. Any resident can come and speak, but only elected members vote.

### Slide 3 — The Town Moderator
Explain the Moderator's job: keeping things fair, making sure everyone gets a turn to speak, calling the vote. Point out that's you. You don't take sides — you're like a referee.

### Slide 4 — Two Big Questions
Transition: two things Natick recently debated — gas leaf blowers and artificial turf fields. Both are real topics people care a lot about.

### Slide 5 — Gas Leaf Blowers
A group called EcoNatick wanted to phase out gas leaf blowers. Why? They're loud. They pollute the air. They can make people sick. They wanted people to switch to electric blowers — or just use a rake!

### Slide 6 — The Debate (Leaf Blowers)
Some people agreed: better for our health and the planet. Some disagreed: electric blowers aren't strong enough yet for big jobs, and new equipment costs a lot. Both sides made good points. Town Meeting listened to everyone.

### Slide 7 — Leaf Blowers Outcome
The vote was 79 yes, 27 no, and 1 person chose not to vote either way — that's called an **abstain**. The "yes" vote meant: send this to a committee to study it more. So no ban for now — just more studying.

### Slide 8 — Artificial Turf
A **moratorium** is a pause. Some residents wanted a three-year pause on building new plastic grass fields. They worried about health and the environment. Others said we need more fields for kids to play on.

### Slide 9 — The Debate (Turf)
One side said: microplastics and forever chemicals could be dangerous. Fields get super hot. The other side said: turf fields can be used way more than grass fields, and we don't have enough fields for all the kids who want to play.

### Slide 10 — Turf Outcome
The vote was 74 yes, 28 no, 3 abstain. The "yes" vote meant: agree to the three-year pause. No new artificial turf fields for three years while the town studies the issue. Existing fields like the Cole Center are fine — they stay.

### Slide 11 — What Can We Learn?
Three big ideas:
- **Anyone can speak up.** You don't have to be a leader. If you care about something, you can share your idea.
- **Debating helps.** Hearing different sides makes us think harder and make better choices.
- **Every vote counts.** Each person gets one vote, and every vote matters. That's democracy.

## Tips

| Tip | |
|-----|--|
| **Pace** | Speak a little slower than normal — 3rd graders need time to process |
| **Words to explain** | moratorium (= a pause), Town Meeting (= where residents vote), Moderator (= the referee), abstain (= choose not to vote yes or no) |
| **Mistakes** | If you stumble, just keep going or do a quick retake |
| **Length** | 2–3 minutes total |

# Video Storyboard: "How Does Town Meeting Work? Two Big Decisions in Natick"

**Audio:** Intro music (fade in/out)

## Slide 2 — What Is Town Meeting?
**Time:** 0:10–0:28 (18s)
**Visual:** Simple diagram: a circle labeled "Town Meeting" with arrows from "Residents" going in, arrow to "Vote" going out. Subtitle: "Towns in Massachusetts let residents vote on big decisions."
**Audio (VO):** "In Massachusetts, many towns don't just let a few leaders make all the choices. Instead, they hold something called Town Meeting. Any resident can come and vote on important questions — like how to spend money, or what rules to make. Think of it like a giant classroom vote, but for the whole town."


## Slide 3 — The Town Moderator
**Time:** 0:28–0:45 (17s)
**Visual:** Photo of Jeff Alderson (Town Moderator) with label: "Jeff Alderson — Natick Town Moderator." Icon of a referee whistle or gavel.
**Audio (VO):** "Town Meeting needs someone to keep things fair and organized. That person is the Town Moderator. In Natick, the Moderator is Jeff Alderson. His job is to make sure everyone gets a chance to speak, to explain the rules, and to call the vote at the end. He doesn't take sides — he's like a referee for the meeting."


## Slide 4 — Two Big Questions
**Time:** 0:45–0:55 (10s)
**Visual:** Two side-by-side icons: a leaf blower icon and a field / grass icon. Text: "In 2024 and 2025, Natick voters debated two big questions."
**Audio (VO):** "At recent Town Meetings, Natick residents debated two big questions. One was about gas-powered leaf blowers. The other was about artificial turf on sports fields. Let's see what happened."


## Slide 5 — Question 1: Gas Leaf Blowers
**Time:** 0:55–1:10 (15s)
**Visual:** Photo of a gas leaf blower in action (credit: Daniel Lobo, CC BY 2.0). Text: "Article 32: Should Natick phase out gas leaf blowers?"
**Audio (VO):** "In 2024, a group called EcoNatick asked Town Meeting to phase out gas-powered leaf blowers. They said the blowers are bad for the environment and people's health — they're loud, they pollute the air, and they make a lot of noise."
**Image:** [`gas_leaf_blower.jpg`](./gas_leaf_blower.jpg) — MUST credit "Daniel Lobo via Wikimedia Commons, CC BY 2.0" in end credits

## Slide 6 — Leaf Blowers: The Debate
**Time:** 1:10–1:28 (18s)
**Visual:** Split screen: "FOR" side (health / environment icons) and "AGAINST" side (landscaper / cost icons). Text bullet: "Supporters said: better for health, quieter neighborhoods. Opponents said: battery tools not ready yet, too expensive for landscaping businesses."
**Audio (VO):** "Supporters said gas leaf blowers cause asthma and noise problems, and electric ones are better. But landscapers said battery-powered blowers just aren't strong enough yet for big jobs. Some Town Meeting members worried it was 'government overreach' — telling people what tools they can use. After a long debate, the town voted to send the question to a committee for more study."


## Slide 7 — Leaf Blowers: The Outcome
**Time:** 1:28–1:38 (10s)
**Visual:** Large text: "Vote: 79-27-1 → Referred for Study" + icon of a document / study group
**Audio (VO):** "The vote was 79 in favor of studying it more, 27 against, and 1 abstention. So the leaf blower question wasn't decided yet — it was sent to a committee to gather more information."


## Slide 8 — Question 2: Artificial Turf
**Time:** 1:38–1:53 (15s)
**Visual:** Photo of Memorial Field artificial turf at Natick High School. Text: "Article 33: Should Natick pause new artificial turf fields?"
**Audio (VO):** "The next year, in 2025, another big question came up. Some residents wanted a three-year pause — called a moratorium — on building any new artificial turf fields in town, like the plastic grass you see on some sports fields."
**Image:** [`artificial_turf_field.jpg`](./artificial_turf_field.jpg) — via Natick Report


## Slide 9 — Artificial Turf: The Debate
**Time:** 1:53–2:18 (25s)
**Visual:** Split screen: "FOR MORATORIUM" (health icons, microplastics, PFAS) and "AGAINST" (soccer/football icons, field shortage). Quote bubbles with short quotes.
**Audio (VO):** "Dr. Roger Luckmann, a doctor from UMass, told Town Meeting that microplastics from artificial turf are ending up in our bodies and could be harmful. He said the plastic blades break down into tiny pieces that can get into the air and water. Other supporters worried about 'forever chemicals' called PFAS, and said the fields get dangerously hot in summer. But youth sports leaders said Natick doesn't have enough fields for kids to play on. Artificial turf can be used much more than grass fields, especially after rain."


## Slide 10 — Artificial Turf: The Outcome
**Time:** 2:18–2:28 (10s)
**Visual:** Large text: "Vote: 74-28-3 → Moratorium PASSED" + green checkmark
**Audio (VO):** "After hearing both sides, Town Meeting voted 74 to 28 to pass the three-year moratorium on new artificial turf. Natick will study the issue before building any more plastic fields."


## Slide 11 — What Can We Learn?
**Time:** 2:28–2:45 (17s)
**Visual:** Three icons with labels: "1. Anyone can speak up", "2. Debates are healthy", "3. Votes are fair"
**Audio (VO):** "So what can we learn from all this? First, in Town Meeting, anyone can bring an idea or speak up about something they care about. Second, it's okay to disagree — debating helps us make better decisions. And third, everyone's vote counts equally. That's democracy in action."
